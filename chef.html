Master Tsumori Hayenami

A Lifetime of Craftsmanship

Tsumori Hayenami (津守 颯波), now a revered 65-year-old sushi master, was born in a small, rural village nestled in the coastal mountains of Japan. His journey into the world of sushi began at the age of 16, when he left his hometown to apprentice under a skilled sushi chef in the bustling city of Osaka. Inspired by the freshness of the sea and the simplicity of life back home, Hayenami immersed himself in the art of sushi, dedicating himself to perfecting the delicate balance of flavor, texture, and presentation.

About Hayenami

With over 50 years of experience, Hayenami’s approach to sushi is deeply rooted in tradition yet nuanced with a personal touch. Every dish he creates reflects his respect for the natural ingredients he works with — from selecting the freshest fish to preparing rice with precise care. His technique, honed over decades, emphasizes the importance of balance, subtlety, and a harmony of flavors.

Hayenami's Heritage

What truly sets Master Hayenami apart is his philosophy of "kodawari" (こだわり) — an unwavering attention to detail and commitment to quality. This dedication is seen in every piece of sushi he crafts, where even the smallest elements are carefully considered to enhance the overall experience.

Tsumori Hayenami’s culinary philosophy is deeply influenced by his upbringing in the mountainous countryside, where his family lived close to the land. Growing up, he learned from his parents and elders the importance of seasonality, foraging, and respecting nature's rhythms. His family’s rural way of life, where they grew their own vegetables and harvested fresh spring water, fostered a deep appreciation for simplicity and the purity of ingredients. This connection to the mountains taught him to approach sushi with the same respect for nature—honoring each ingredient’s natural state and bringing out its essence. His sushi is a reflection of his mountain heritage, combining the purity of rural life with the freshness of the sea, resulting in a style that is both grounded in tradition and uniquely his own.

Though he’s become a master of his craft, Tsumori remains humble, often reflecting on his roots in the countryside, where nature taught him the value of patience, respect, and simplicity. Today, his creations are as much an expression of his life’s journey as they are of his deep connection to tradition.

Tsumori Hayenami’s work is not just about creating sushi — it’s about sharing a piece of his soul with every bite, inviting those who taste his food to savor not just the dish but the story behind it. His legacy is one of craftsmanship, passion, and a lifelong pursuit of culinary excellence.
